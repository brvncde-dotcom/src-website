# SRC Membership Model — Cross-Verification Results

## High Confidence Findings (Confirmed by ≥2 dimensions with independent sources)

1. **"Open by default" is the think tank norm** — Dim01, Dim02, Dim11 confirm: Brookings, Carnegie, ECFR, CSIS, RAND all make core research freely available. Paid tiers differentiate through experience, not basic content access.
2. **Registration walls convert 16x better than newsletter forms** — Dim01, Dim10 confirm: Salem Reporter test, Piano research. 20% of registered users eventually convert to paid.
3. **Direct analyst access is the #1 premium differentiator** — Dim03, Dim04, Dim11 confirm: GLG charges $1,000-$2,500/hr. RANE Enterprise, Oxford Analytica CCM, and Chatham House Major Corporate all use it as top-tier anchor.
4. **Tier naming matters: "Basic" signals inadequacy** — Dim02, Dim08, Dim11 confirm: Value-based names (Essential/Core/Professional) convert 15-30% better. GCTI's SIGNAL/SENTINEL/SOVEREIGN branding demonstrates creative differentiation.
5. **Annual billing at 17% discount ("2 months free") is optimal** — Dim08, Dim10 confirm: 92% retention vs. 68% for monthly. 40-71% higher LTV. Annual default increases adoption 2-3x.
6. **The $29-39/month entry point is market-validated** — Dim02, Dim08, Dim11 confirm: RANE $32/mo, GCTI AEGIS Pro $29/mo, Geopolitical Desk £29/mo (~$37/mo).
7. **Newsletters are the primary conversion vehicle** — Dim01, Dim07, Dim10 confirm: NYT's "The Morning" 42% free-to-paid conversion. FT newsletter access drives Premium retention. 3-5% is standard B2B free-to-paid conversion.
8. **No major think tank has deployed AI avatar video briefings** — Dim05, Dim11 confirm: First-mover opportunity is genuine. Technology (Synthesia, HeyGen) is production-ready at 70-99% cost reduction vs. traditional video.
9. **Swiss neutrality commands premium positioning** — Dim08, Dim11 confirm: Switzerland ranks highest GDP/capita, consumers normalize elevated prices. Strict privacy laws and perceived independence are monetizable differentiators.
10. **Time-gated release (reverse paywall) works** — Dim04, Dim06 confirm: BoiseDev 24h timewall, Mittmedia 20% conversion from reverse paywall. Premium-first content flow is industry standard.

## Medium Confidence (Single authoritative source)

1. **vAvatar optimal length: 90-120 seconds for daily briefs** — Dim05: 70% retention at this length. Weekly analysis: 7-10 min.
2. **PDF downloads are a key conversion driver for B2B** — Dim02: RANE explicitly includes in Basic tier. Tangible artifact for workflow integration.
3. **Community access drives 4x higher ARPU** — Dim03: ClubGPF model ($180 vs $45). But this is a single-source claim.
4. **The geopolitical risk analytics market is $4.02B, growing at 14.3% CAGR** — Dim11: Single industry report. Directionally useful but unverified.
5. **AI-personalized video achieves 200-300% higher response rates** — Dim05: Marketing industry data, not think tank-specific.

## Conflict Zones

1. **Optimal number of tiers**: Dim02 recommends 3 paid tiers (Free→Essential→Professional→Executive); Dim03 researcher suggested 3-tier (Observer→Analyst→Executive). But SRC explicitly requested 4 tiers (Free/Basic/Premium/Expert). Resolution: Keep 4 tiers but use value-based naming.
2. **Free tier archive depth**: Dim01 recommends 6 months; Dim02 suggests 12-24 months for Basic tier. Resolution: 6 months for anonymous, 12 months for registered free users.
3. **Mobile app necessity**: Dim12 says "native app only at 5,000+ members"; Dim06 notes RANE reserves app for enterprise tier. Resolution: Start with responsive web + PWA; native app for Expert/Enterprise only.
