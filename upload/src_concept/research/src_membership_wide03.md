# Expert Access & Video Briefing Monetization — Research Report

**Date:** July 2025
**Researcher:** Competitive Intelligence Team
**Focus:** How think tanks, intelligence services, and expert networks monetize live expert access, video briefings, Q&A sessions, and exclusive interactive content — for SRC's "Expert" tier design.

---

## Facet: Expert Access & Video Briefing Monetization

### Key Findings

**1. Think Tank Corporate Memberships as the Primary Monetization Vehicle for Expert Access**

Think tanks overwhelmingly monetize expert access through tiered corporate membership programs rather than per-session pricing. These programs bundle briefings, events, private dinners, and direct analyst access into annual fees.

- **CSIS** operates three invitation-only membership groups: International Councillors (highest level, established by Henry Kissinger in 1979), Advisory Board (co-chaired by Carla Hills and Joseph Dunford, meets twice yearly), and Roundtables (periodic meetings between members and CSIS experts) [^27^]. All are invitation-only and pricing is not public, but the International Councillors includes global business leaders like Richard Li (Pacific Century Group), Rex Tillerson, and Ray Hunt [^139^].

- **CFR** Corporate membership is divided into three levels: "Founders" ($100,000/year), "President's Circle" ($60,000/year), and "Affiliates" ($30,000/year). President's Circle and Founders receive attendance at "small, private dinners or receptions with senior American officials and world leaders" [^25^].

- **Chatham House** offers Standard Corporate Membership at GBP £4,500/year (~$5,700) and Major Corporate Membership (pricing not public). Major Corporate Members receive "regular briefings on emerging geopolitical risk, held under Chatham House rule," "exclusive discussions, dinners and networking receptions," and 60% conference discounts. Standard members get networking receptions and invitations to 120+ on-record events [^33^] [^35^] [^113^].

- **Atlantic Council** corporate program has four paths: Strategic Partnership ($250,000+), Program Partnership ($25,000-$100,000+), Event Sponsorship ($25,000-$100,000+), and Corporate Membership ($25,000-$100,000+). Benefits include "invitations to private dinners, gatherings, discussions, and partners-only conference calls," "private briefings from our leading experts," and "an annual, partners-only roundtable discussion with Atlantic Council President and CEO" [^54^] [^265^]. Individual annual membership tiers: Councilor ($5,000), Senior Councilor ($10,000), Global Councilor ($25,000) [^53^].

- **Brookings Institution** revenue was $89.4 million in 2023 with a $538 million endowment [^270^]. Corporate partnerships are structured around research program support with private briefing access for senior partners.

**2. Expert Networks Charge $900-$1,500/Hour for Live Expert Access — With Subscription Models Dominating**

Expert networks (GLG, AlphaSights, Third Bridge, Guidepoint) represent the most mature market for monetized expert access, offering direct comparables for SRC's Expert tier.

- **Pricing benchmarks:** Most expert networks charge $900-$1,500 per hour of expert consultation [^82^] [^85^]. GLG and AlphaSights are at the premium end ($1,000-$1,500/hr), while Third Bridge and Tegus/AlphaSense tend to be lower for transcript-heavy users [^82^]. Mid-level experts cost $300-$600/hr, senior executives $600-$1,200/hr, and highly specialized experts $1,200-$2,000+/hr [^88^]. A standard 45-60 minute expert call falls within $400-$1,500 depending on preparation, screening, and compliance requirements [^88^].

- **GLG** (founded 1998): Largest network with 900,000+ vetted experts. Annual subscriptions range from $60,000 to several hundred thousand dollars for enterprise clients. Individual calls priced $1,000-$2,500/hour. Premium pricing due to institutional compliance infrastructure (50+ compliance staff) [^84^] [^85^].

- **AlphaSights** (founded 2008): Rapid expert matching, high-touch service. Pricing ~5-10% lower than GLG. Credit-based or subscription. Premium pricing for time-sensitive or senior-expert requests [^84^] [^85^].

- **Third Bridge** (founded 2007): Combines analyst-led transcript library (Forum) with live expert sourcing. Subscription-based, bundling library access with live call credits. MCP server for Claude AI integration — 90,000 expert transcripts [^85^].

- **Credit-based model trap:** Many networks charge 1.5-3x "credits" per call depending on expert seniority, making true hourly cost 30-50% higher than headline credit prices [^82^] [^86^]. Clients should always ask for "median credits charged per 1-hour call at my expert seniority level" [^82^].

- **Expert compensation:** Experts typically receive $200-$1,000+/hr, representing only 30-40% of client fees. The broker markup (60-70%) covers network maintenance, account management, compliance, platform technology, and profit margins of 30-45% [^84^].

**3. Video Briefing Models: The Economist Insider as the Flagship Template**

The Economist's "Insider" (launched October 2025) represents the most ambitious media-video-geopolitics hybrid product and offers a direct template for SRC's vAvatar concept.

- **Structure:** Twice-weekly shows hosted by senior editors. The main show every Thursday hosted by editor-in-chief Zanny Minton Beddoes and deputy editor Edward Carr. Four monthly themed shows: Inside Defence (Shashank Joshi), Inside Geopolitics (David Rennie), Inside Economics (Rachana Shanbhogue, Henry Curr, Mike Bird), Inside Tech (Tom Standage, Alex Hern) [^91^] [^94^].

- **Guest access:** Extended guest interviews with world leaders, thinkers, and policymakers who debate views with Economist journalists — including "prominent voices who disagree with The Economist's editorial positions" [^94^].

- **Interactivity:** Subscribers can submit questions in advance. Regular "Insider Q&A shows" and a dedicated Insider newsletter three times per week [^95^].

- **Distribution:** Episodes stream Tuesdays and Thursdays, then available on-demand. Smaller cutdown versions on YouTube (3.6M subscribers) [^95^].

- **Pricing:** Free to existing subscribers "for the foreseeable future" — bundled as a retention tool rather than a separate revenue stream [^91^]. This signals that video content is increasingly a table-stakes feature for premium subscriptions.

**4. Foreign Policy's FP Live and Insider Access — Tiered Video Q&A Model**

Foreign Policy offers a differentiated two-tier model for live expert video access that provides direct comparables for SRC.

- **FP Live:** Regular video interviews with world leaders (David Petraeus, Ehud Barak, Fiona Hill, Larry Summers, Rafael Grossi, etc.) hosted by FP's Ravi Agrawal. All subscribers can watch on-demand [^302^].

- **FP Insider tier** (premium upgrade): Adds "Insider Access calls" — special live video debriefs with FP reporters and editors (e.g., Munich Security Conference debrief, NATO summit debrief). Only FP Insiders can submit questions for FP Live interviews [^302^] [^304^]. Additional benefits: Insider-only articles, FP Live transcripts, ad-free browsing, curated reading lists, direct line to FP staff [^304^].

- **Frequency:** Multiple FP Live events per month, plus quarterly or event-triggered Insider Access calls [^302^].

**5. RANE/Stratfor: Tiered Intelligence Subscription with Analyst Access as Premium Feature**

RANE (which acquired Stratfor in 2020) offers the clearest tiered pricing model for geopolitical intelligence subscriptions.

- **WorldView Basic:** <$8/week (~$32/month). Limited to 10 situation reports/analyses per month. Daily Content Brief, Weekly Rundown, podcasts, maps/charts [^59^].

- **WorldView Essential:** Starting at $31/week (~$124/month). Unlimited situation reports and analysis. Quarterly and yearly geopolitical forecast summaries. Sector-specific analysis [^59^].

- **Geopolitical Intelligence** (enterprise tier): Custom pricing. Adds annual/quarterly forecasts for global, regional, country-level outlooks; enterprise forecasting tools; scenario tracking; Daily/Weekly Intelligence Briefs; AI reporting tools; mobile app; API access; **"Direct Analyst access for deeper insights"** [^59^].

- **Stratfor legacy pricing:** Annual subscriptions as low as $99 through partnership discounts (WACA), with regular pricing at ~$7/week for Basic [^144^] [^266^]. Enterprise clients receive tailored intelligence services including custom forecasting [^144^].

- **RANE Risk Intelligence** (separate product): Multi-seat subscriptions, optional analyst access, dedicated account management, client-specific intel briefs, bi-annual Network Intelligence Report [^242^].

- **Oxford Analytica:** Comprehensive Client Membership documented at $14,200 AUD for government clients [^240^].

**6. World Economic Forum Digital Membership — Virtual Access to Expert Briefings**

WEF offers a purely digital membership tier that provides virtual access to expert content — relevant for SRC's virtual-only tier thinking.

- **Premium:** EUR 25.50/month (annual). Access to exclusive virtual events and expert briefings, on-demand library, PDF briefings on 300+ topics, AI map assistant, weekly newsletter [^292^] [^295^].

- **Pro:** EUR 76.50/month (annual). Adds enhanced AI-generated briefings, unlimited intelligence maps, shareable maps, networking hub, expert-led masterclasses with certificates [^292^] [^295^].

- **Special rates for teams and institutions** including universities and think tanks [^292^].

- **Digital Members Track at Davos:** Pro and Premium members get access to exclusive virtual programme during Annual Meeting with curated livestreams [^298^].

**7. AI Avatar / Virtual Presenter Technology — Enterprise-Ready at Scale**

AI avatar technology has matured significantly and is now enterprise-ready for video content production at a fraction of traditional costs.

- **Synthesia** (founded 2017, $150M+ funding): Enterprise-first. Starter $22/mo (10 min video), Creator $67/mo (30 min), Enterprise custom. 240+ avatars, 160+ languages. SOC 2, GDPR, ISO 42001 certified. Custom avatars $1,000/year. SCORM and 1-click translation locked behind Enterprise tier [^149^] [^152^].

- **HeyGen** (founded 2020): Creative/marketing focused. Creator $24/mo, Business $149/mo + $20/seat. 1,100+ avatars, 175+ languages with native lip sync. Custom avatars $99 one-time (~90% cheaper than Synthesia). SOC 2 Type II, GDPR, CCPA. 4K export on Pro+. AI Playground with Google Veo 3.1 B-roll [^149^] [^152^].

- **Key insight for SRC:** A 25-person team using HeyGen Business pays ~$7,548/year vs. Synthesia Enterprise at $15,000-$25,000+/year [^154^]. AI video production costs are now $24-$149/month vs. traditional video production at $1,000-$10,000+ per segment [^110^].

- **Enterprise AI video cost:** $8,000-$50,000+/year for comprehensive enterprise use, still dramatically cheaper than traditional studio production which requires "presenters, studio equipment, lighting, cameras, editors, and production staff" running "thousands of dollars for a single segment" [^110^].

**8. CNBC Pro — Business Media Video Subscription Benchmark**

CNBC's premium video subscription provides a B2B media pricing benchmark.

- **CNBC Pro:** $299.99/year or $34.99/month. Includes live TV feed (US, Europe, Asia), subscriber-only newsletter, original articles/videos with exclusive insight, "Pro Talk" videos with major investors [^296^] [^297^].

- **CNBC Investing Club:** $400/year. Jim Cramer's market analysis, trade alerts, monthly live meetings [^294^].

- **Growth:** CNBC tripled active paid subscribers year-over-year; 63% from organic CNBC funnel [^294^].

- **Key tactic:** 7-day free trial outperformed 30-day trial for conversion. Price testing and promotional offers (e.g., $59.99 for 3 months) drove 2x monthly new subscriber averages [^297^].

**9. B2B Intelligence Video Content Market Size and Trends**

The broader B2B media market validates the commercial opportunity for professional video content.

- Global B2B media market: ~$28.2 billion in 2026, growing at 6.8% CAGR [^114^].

- Revenue breakdown: Subscription 36.2%, Advertising 29.4%, Events 24.8%, Data/API licensing 9.6% [^114^].

- Subscription model growing fastest at 9.1% CAGR through 2034 [^114^].

- SME-oriented B2B subscriptions priced $19-$299/month [^114^].

- B2B CPMs: $40-$180 vs. $2-$15 for consumer media [^114^].

- 1.4 billion unique professional readers on digital B2B platforms in 2025 [^114^].

**10. B2B Virtual Roundtables as a Monetization Format**

Virtual roundtables represent a proven format for monetizing expert access in B2B contexts.

- Standard format: 90-minute moderated sessions with one host/representative and 6 senior decision-makers [^89^].

- Value proposition: "Bespoke, closed-door, invitation only" sessions with "prequalified audience of targeted executives" [^89^].

- Lead generation model: Delegate acquisition teams secure participation, gather intelligence, and schedule follow-up 1:1 meetings [^89^].

- For think tanks: LEK Consulting hosted virtual roundtables with 13 pricing experts for peer exchange on B2B SaaS monetization [^279^].

**11. Advisory Retainer Models — Monthly Access Pricing**

Consulting retainer models offer a framework for recurring expert access pricing.

- Advisory retainers with monthly office hours: $2,000-$5,000/month [^282^].
- Ongoing strategic advisory: $3,000-$8,500/month [^283^].
- Fractional CSO retainer: $5,500-$15,000/month [^283^].
- Board/executive advisory: $2,000-$5,500/month [^283^].
- Boutique consultants: $2,000-$8,000/month [^285^].
- Large consulting firms: $15,000+/month [^285^].
- The "5X Rule": consultants aim to deliver 5x the value of their fee [^287^].

**12. Fastlane's "Intelligence Briefing" — Novel Free-to-Access Model**

A boutique positioning firm offers free 45-minute private briefings as a lead qualification tool — demonstrating that expert access can be a loss-leader for high-value services [^269^].

- 45-minute private call with a principal
- Principal reviews public materials in advance
- One-page memo within 48 hours after the call
- Cost: "Nothing. Genuinely."
- "We do roughly six a week. We turn down two-thirds of the businesses we brief."
- The screening IS the point: "The briefing is the only way to talk to us. We don't take RFPs, we don't run a sales team."

### Trends & Signals

1. **Video content is becoming table stakes for premium subscriptions.** The Economist bundled Insider free to existing subscribers; Foreign Policy uses video as a tier differentiator; RANE produces regular podcasts and video. Video is no longer a luxury add-on but a core retention tool [^91^] [^302^].

2. **Tiered access is the dominant model.** Every major player (RANE, WEF, FP, CFR, Atlantic Council, Chatham House) uses tiered pricing with expert access reserved for upper tiers. This creates clear upgrade incentives [^59^] [^53^] [^292^] [^302^].

3. **"Direct analyst access" is the universal premium feature.** RANE explicitly calls this out as the top-tier differentiator. Expert networks charge 2-5x for live access vs. written content. Personal interaction commands the highest premium [^59^] [^88^].

4. **AI avatar technology enables scalable "expert presence."** At $24-$149/month, AI avatars can deliver consistent, branded video content without production costs. 175+ language support enables global reach. Quality is now photorealistic at close range [^149^] [^110^].

5. **Hybrid virtual/in-person models are standard.** WEF offers digital-only membership with virtual event access. Chatham House gives virtual access to conferences. CSIS has both in-person and virtual roundtables. The pandemic permanently normalized virtual as a legitimate tier [^33^] [^292^] [^142^].

6. **Freemium as acquisition tool, premium for expert access.** RANE's Basic tier (<$8/week) converts to Essential ($31/week) and then to enterprise. WEF's Premium converts to Pro. FP's standard subscription upgrades to Insider. All use content access as the conversion lever [^59^] [^292^] [^302^].

7. **Q&A and question-submission as engagement hooks.** The Economist lets subscribers submit questions in advance. FP only lets Insiders submit questions for live interviews. This creates scarcity and drives upgrades [^95^] [^302^].

8. **B2B media subscriptions are the fastest-growing segment.** 9.1% CAGR through 2034, with enterprises demonstrating willingness to pay premium prices for "ad-free, deeply specialized, and frequently updated professional intelligence" [^114^].

9. **Expert networks are expanding beyond 1:1 calls.** Third Bridge's MCP server for Claude AI, Tegus/AlphaSense's 200K+ transcript library, and GLG's growing archive all point toward a hybrid model: AI-searchable recorded content + live custom calls [^85^].

10. **"Client-only calls" as institutional exclusivity markers.** Oxford Analytica, Chatham House, and private intelligence firms use invitation-only or client-only calls as signals of premium status. This scarcity drives retention [^240^] [^33^].

### Controversies & Conflicting Claims

1. **AI avatar authenticity vs. human credibility.** AI avatars dramatically reduce production costs ($24/month vs. thousands per segment), but the same article warns of "synthetic media and information warfare" — AI-generated personas like "Wolf News" promoting geopolitical narratives [^110^]. For SRC, the question is: can AI-delivered intelligence maintain the same credibility as human experts? CITE's experience in Zimbabwe found that AI avatars can "hold power to account in new ways" but faced criticism about replacing women's jobs and struggles with local accents [^111^].

2. **Credit-based pricing opacity in expert networks.** GLG, AlphaSights, and Guidepoint use credit systems where "the stated price per credit understates the true cost" by 30-50%. Critics call this deliberate opacity; defenders say it enables tiered expert pricing [^82^] [^84^] [^86^]. Third-party marketplace Inex One ($1,050/hr pay-as-you-go) positions itself as the transparent alternative [^83^].

3. **Should expert access be free as a lead gen tool or paid as premium?** Fastlane offers free 45-minute briefings as "the only way to talk to us" — using scarcity as a screen [^269^]. Conversely, GLG charges up to $2,500/hour. The market spans both extremes. For SRC, the question is whether video briefings are an acquisition tool (freemium) or a premium revenue stream.

4. **The Economist Insider is free to subscribers.** Despite significant production investment (twice-weekly shows, multiple senior editors, world leaders as guests), The Economist chose not to charge separately [^91^]. This suggests video content may be a retention/competitive tool rather than a standalone revenue driver.

5. **Is the expert network model extractive?** Critics note that experts receive only 30-40% of client fees, that networks intentionally limit direct client-expert relationships to maintain dependency, and that the same expert may be available through multiple networks at different prices [^84^]. This creates market opportunity for platforms that offer fairer revenue splits or direct expert-booking models.

### Recommended Deep-Dive Areas

1. **Optimal pricing architecture for SRC's Expert tier:** Given the benchmarks — RANE Essential at $31/week ($124/month), WEF Pro at EUR 76.50/month, FP Insider at likely $15-25/month premium over standard, expert networks at $1,000+/hr — where should SRC position? The $50-$150/month range for serious individual professionals and $500-$2,000/month for institutional tiers appears to be the sweet spot.

2. **Video frequency and format optimization:** The Economist's twice-weekly cadence with monthly themed shows represents the most ambitious model. FP's on-demand + event-triggered model is more sustainable for smaller teams. What frequency can SRC realistically deliver? Weekly? Bi-weekly? What is the marginal retention value of each additional video?

3. **AI avatar integration for the vAvatar concept:** What is the optimal mix of AI-avatar-delivered content (scalable, low-cost, 24/7 available) vs. live human expert access (high-credibility, high-scarcity, premium-priced)? Should AI avatars handle daily briefings while live experts handle monthly deep-dives?

4. **Live Q&A mechanics:** The Economist's advance question submission, FP's Insider-only question submission, and RANE's "Direct Analyst access" represent different Q&A models. Which maximizes both engagement and upgrade conversion? Should live Q&A be the primary differentiator of the Expert tier?

5. **Institutional vs. individual tier design:** CFR's model ($30K-$100K corporate) and RANE's model ($32/month-$custom enterprise) show radically different approaches. Should SRC offer both? What institutional benefits (multi-seat, API access, custom briefings) justify 10-100x pricing multiples?

6. **"Client-only calls" exclusivity mechanics:** How can SRC replicate the exclusivity and prestige of Oxford Analytica's client-only calls or Chatham House's Chatham Rule briefings? Is it about content scarcity (not available elsewhere), speaker scarcity (world leaders), or access scarcity (limited seats)?

7. **Virtual roundtable monetization:** B2B virtual roundtables charge $5,000-$50,000+ per session as lead generation tools. Could SRC monetize virtual roundtables as a standalone product? What is the market for "6 executives + 1 SRC expert" sessions?

8. **Advisory retainer model for enterprise clients:** Strategy consulting retainers at $5,000-$15,000/month for ongoing advisory suggest that SRC could offer monthly retainer tiers with guaranteed analyst access hours, custom briefings, and priority response.

9. **On-demand vs. live content balance:** The Economist offers both (stream live + on-demand). WEF emphasizes on-demand library. RANE produces daily briefs + podcasts. What mix of synchronous (live, high-engagement) vs. asynchronous (on-demand, scalable) maximizes both retention and revenue?

10. **Geographic and language expansion:** HeyGen's 175+ languages and Synthesia's 140+ languages enable global video delivery at marginal cost. Should SRC offer multilingual vAvatar briefings? What is the incremental value vs. cost?
