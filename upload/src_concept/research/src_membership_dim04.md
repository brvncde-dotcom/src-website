## Facet: Expert Tier — Live Access, Video Calls & VIP Experience

### Key Findings

- **FP Insider's Q&A model is the benchmark for tiered expert access**: Foreign Policy's "FP Insider" tier differentiates through the ability for subscribers to "submit questions to expert guests on FP Live" and join "Insider Access calls with FP's reporters and editors" — while basic subscribers can only watch live. [^477^] [^304^] Only FP subscribers can submit questions for FP Live interviews; non-subscribers can watch but not participate. [^519^] This creates a clear exclusivity ladder: watch (free) → submit questions (subscriber) → join insider calls + get transcripts (Insider tier). [^304^]

- **Expert network pricing validates the value of direct analyst access**: GLG charges clients $1,500-$2,000 per hour for expert calls, with annual enterprise contracts ranging from $50,000 to $500,000+ per year. [^569^] [^431^] The expert receives only 30-40% of this fee; the broker (GLG) captures 60-70% as markup for access, compliance, and matching. [^84^] This pricing architecture confirms that direct expert access commands the highest willingness-to-pay in the knowledge economy. [^88^]

- **Think tank top-tier pricing anchors at $25K-$100K annually**: CFR's corporate tiers are Founders ($100,000+), President's Circle ($60,000+), and Affiliates ($30,000+). [^25^] [^432^] Atlantic Council's Global Councilor tier is priced at $25,000 with a private meeting with program directors. [^53^] Aspen Society's Gold Leaf tier was raised from $50K to $75K in 2024, Chairman's Society from $25K to $35K. [^526^] [^528^]

- **RANE's enterprise model shows how "optional analyst access" scales**: RANE offers multi-seat subscriptions with "optional analyst access," "dedicated account management," and "client-specific intel briefs" as enterprise-tier features atop their standard platform. - **Oxford Analytica Daily Brief provides a model for breaking news flash alerts**: The Daily Brief delivers intelligence in "full-length analysis, concise summaries, visual data, infographics, online briefings and access to our analysts" across 130+ countries. - **Global Risk Outlook (GIG) structures a three-speed alert system**: Daily risk alerts on breaking events, weekly forecasts of near-term trends, and quarterly strategic outlooks — all with "analyst-led delivery" where "subscribers can request clarification at any time." [^76^]

- **Early access windows of 48-72 hours are standard practice**: Podcast subscriptions typically offer early access up to 2 weeks prior to public release; [^461^] the premium subscriber model of "episodes 48 hours earlier" is common across media. [^547^] Oxford Analytica offers "client-only calls" with early access to their analysis. [^38^]

### Trends & Signals

- **Direct analyst access is becoming the primary differentiator**: RANE's "optional analyst access," Oxford Analytica's "client-only calls," and GIG's "subscribers can request clarification at any time" all signal a shift toward direct human access as the premium feature. [^242^] [^323^] [^76^] Research shows this is the #1 enterprise differentiator for expert networks. [^569^]

- **Tiered video/live access is becoming standard**: FP's three-tier live video model (watch → submit questions → join insider calls) mirrors how think tanks should structure their live access tiers. [^519^] [^304^] CSIS Roundtables and CFR's private dinners show the in-person equivalent. [^27^] [^564^]

- **"Under the Chatham House Rule" briefings create irreplaceable value**: Chatham House's explicit branding of briefings as held under their Rule creates a format competitors cannot replicate. [^33^] Eurasia Group's "off-the-record" roundtables follow the same logic. [^556^]

- **Annual price increases across top think tanks**: Aspen Society raised prices for the first time since 2005 in 2024 (Gold Leaf: $50K→$75K, Chairman's: $25K→$35K). [^526^] CFR's President's Circle rose from $50K to $60K. [^432^] Signal: the market can bear higher prices for exclusive access.

- **Flash brief format is converging on 150-300 words with analyst commentary**: Oxford Analytica's concise summaries, RANE's RiskBook "daily" format, and GIG's "time-critical notifications" all follow a short-form, analyst-annotated model designed for mobile consumption. [^323^] [^242^] [^76^]

- **Enterprise features increasingly include collaboration tools**: RANE's enterprise tier adds "collaboration tools to share and comment on content," "Smart Report AI auto-summarization," and team features — signaling the importance of team-based intelligence workflows. [^242^]

- **Personalized/dashboard delivery is the new baseline**: Oxford Analytica's "personalised alerts," WEF's "customized intelligence map," and Polaris's "personalised dashboard" all show that one-size-fits-all briefings are being replaced by personalized intelligence. [^323^] [^532^] [^571^]

- **Breaking news alert delivery prioritizes speed and analyst vetting**: GIG's alerts are "vetted by analysts for relevance to your sector or region" — not automated. [^76^] The RANE RiskBook is "curated" daily. [^242^] AI-powered alert systems (like Polaris) are emerging but human curation remains the premium signal. [^571^]

- **Cohort scarcity drives application**: The Atlantic Council's "competitive nomination process" and CSIS's "invitation-only" structure create perceived scarcity. [^53^] [^27^] WEF's invitation-only membership model is the extreme example. [^533^]

- **Early access windows are expanding**: While 48-72 hours was the podcast/media standard, [^547^] Oxford Analytica offers privileged subscribers early access to analysis ahead of public release, [^323^] and some platforms offer up to 2 weeks early access for premium subscribers. [^461^]

### Controversies & Conflicting Claims

- **How much direct access is too much?**: GLG's model of $1,500-$2,000/hr creates a barrier that many clients find excessive. [^431^] Some clients report "mismatch of skills" leading to "less productive calls." [^431^] Meanwhile, platforms like Inex One offer pay-as-you-go at $1,050/hr without prepayment, [^83^] suggesting that the high-prepayment model may face disruption.

- **Invitation-only vs. open application**: CSIS's "invitation-only" approach [^27^] contrasts with Atlantic Council's "competitive nomination process" where applicants can self-nominate. [^53^] The question is whether invitation-only creates better cohort quality or merely signals exclusivity artificially.

- **Virtual vs. in-person for VIP experience**: The WSJ reported on "ultraexclusive conferences" in Sicily, Aspen, and Stockholm where "Elon Musk and Margot Robbie mingle with bank leaders and media moguls" — suggesting that in-person exclusivity commands a premium that virtual cannot replicate. [^568^] However, FP Live's entirely virtual model demonstrates that expert access can scale digitally. [^519^]

- **Flash brief depth vs. speed trade-off**: GIG offers "time-critical notifications" with analyst vetting, [^76^] while automated AI systems (like Polaris) can deliver instant alerts at $9/month. [^571^] The risk is that speed without analyst depth creates noise rather than signal.

- **Credit-based pricing transparency**: GLG and Third Bridge use opaque "credit" systems where "many expert networks routinely charge multiple credits per expert call," making the true price higher than advertised. [^83^] This creates potential trust issues with members.

- **Breaking news delivery channel fatigue**: Push notifications have high opt-in but rapid fatigue; [^539^] email remains reliable but slow; SMS has highest open rates but is expensive. [^535^] The consensus is a multi-channel approach: push for breaking, email for daily briefs, SMS only for critical alerts.
