# Executive Summary

## Key Findings

SRC Advisory occupies a vacant competitive position at the intersection of three forces no rival has combined: Swiss neutrality as a trust anchor, AI-augmented delivery at production-ready cost, and tiered membership architecture validated across the intelligence subscription market. The geopolitical risk analytics market reached $4.02 billion in 2025 and is projected to grow at 14.3% CAGR to $15.26 billion by 2035[^1005^]. Europe holds 27.5% of that market, yet incumbents — RANE WorldView, Oxford Analytica, POLITICO Pro, and Chatham House — remain overwhelmingly Anglo-American in framing. No major think tank has deployed AI avatar video briefings at scale, despite technology from Synthesia and HeyGen cutting video production costs by 70–99% versus traditional studio production[^579^][^574^]. This is not a capability gap; it is a first-mover window measured in months, not years.

The competitive audit reveals a structural opening. RANE charges $28–80 per month for individual subscriptions and reserves direct analyst access for enterprise tiers[^59^][^274^]. POLITICO Pro commands $35,000–85,000 annually for institutional clients and sustains a 90% renewal rate by embedding intelligence into legislative workflows[^249^][^250^]. Expert networks such as GLG charge $900–1,500 per hour for analyst consultations[^82^]. None offers tiered access that graduates an individual from free discovery to live analyst engagement within one architecture. SRC's model treats membership as a progression of relationship depth rather than a binary paywall.

The revenue psychology is equally compelling. Registration walls convert at 16 times the rate of passive newsletter forms, with 20% of registered users eventually upgrading to paid. Value-based tier naming outperforms generic labels by 15–30% in conversion[^208^]. Annual billing at a 17% discount delivers 92% twelve-month retention versus 68% for monthly plans, while generating 40–71% higher lifetime value[^762^]. These mechanics compound: a registered user who converts to annual Essential and upgrades to Professional within six months delivers roughly 8 times the revenue of a direct monthly purchaser.

The vAvatar video briefing system is the single most distinctive element. At 90–120 seconds for daily briefings, AI avatar video achieves approximately 70% viewer retention — substantially above text-based alternatives[^580^]. Synthesia's European-language lip-sync quality (4.1/5 for French and German) makes it the platform of choice for SRC's quadrilingual market[^633^]. The same script delivered in German, French, Italian, and English at marginal cost turns Switzerland's linguistic diversity from operational burden into competitive moat. Framing output as "AI-delivered, expert-curated" mitigates the documented trust penalty of AI-generated content while preserving scale[^636^].

## The 4-Tier Model at a Glance

Each tier is designed around a specific relationship depth, with content format — not merely volume — as the primary differentiator.

| Dimension | Observer (Free) | Essential (CHF 29/mo) | Professional (CHF 79/mo) | Executive (CHF 149/mo) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Relationship depth** | Discovery | Routine consumer | Serious practitioner | Institutional decision-maker |
| **Core content** | Opinions, briefs, newsletters, podcasts | Unlimited reports, PDF downloads, daily brief, 12-month archive | Exclusive studies, vAvatar videos, early access (24h), weekly briefing | Live analyst calls, breaking news alerts, annual Switzerland summit, bespoke research |
| **Video access** | 60–90 second public clips | Full 2-minute daily briefings | Full briefings + expert commentary, 15-minute deep dives | All premium content + live briefings + on-demand analyst Q&A |
| **Speed of delivery** | Weekly digest | Next-day analysis | Same-day briefing | Real-time flash alert + analyst response within 4 hours |
| **Event access** | Public events only | Monthly virtual briefing | Weekly virtual + quarterly private roundtable | Weekly live calls + annual invitation-only summit |
| **Archive depth** | Last 6 months | 12 months | 24 months | Full archive + custom research |
| **Audio layer** | Selected articles | Audio newsletters | Narrated full reports | Podcast-style analyst interviews, ad-free |
| **Billing** | Registration-gated | Monthly or annual (17% discount) | Monthly or annual (17% discount) | Annual-only; corporate CHF 5,000–25,000/year |

The pricing architecture exploits the compromise effect: the second-paid tier (Professional at CHF 79) captures the majority of serious subscribers by sitting between a limited entry point (Essential) and an anchor tier (Executive) that makes the mid-tier appear exceptional in value[^831^]. This price point sits within the validated market band — RANE's equivalent costs approximately $80 per month; GCTI's professional platform commands $99 per month[^274^][^208^].

The conversion funnel is deliberately inverted. Anonymous visitors hit a registration wall after 2–3 articles, not a paywall, capturing email for nurture while preserving content discovery. A 10-day free trial on paid tiers — no credit card required — maintains the urgency that drives conversion; users who complete key activation actions during trial convert at 3–5 times the rate of non-activated users[^758^]. Every paid tier carries a 30-day money-back guarantee, shown to lift conversion 10–20% with minimal refund rate increase[^764^].

The Executive tier is anchored by an annual invitation-only summit in Switzerland, modelled on Chatham House Rule gatherings that justify premium memberships at Atlantic Council ($5,000–25,000/year) and Chatham House (£345/year)[^29^][^53^]. Corporate licences at CHF 5,000–25,000 annually provide 5–50 nominated individual accounts, making the corporate sale about "seats at the table" rather than content access. Churn becomes costly in institutional relationships, not merely subscription fees.

The chapters that follow unpack each dimension: competitive positioning against RANE, Oxford Analytica, and European think tanks; the "reverse timewall" content cascade; pricing psychology and Swiss market specifics; newsletter strategy as the primary conversion vehicle; vAvatar technical implementation; and the complete conversion funnel from anonymous visitor to Executive member.
