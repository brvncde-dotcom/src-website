## Facet: Free Tier Content Architecture

### Key Findings

#### 1. Think Tank "Open by Default" Model Dominates

- **Brookings Institution**: All research articles, reports, and commentary freely accessible without registration. No paywall on any research content. Funding comes from donations, grants, and institutional support [^549^]
- **Carnegie Endowment for International Peace**: All articles, commentaries, and research freely available. No registration required for content access [^554^]
- **ECFR (European Council on Foreign Relations)**: All policy reports, briefs, and memos downloadable for free. Newsletters, podcasts, and articles all openly accessible [^196^] [^147^]
- **CSIS (Center for Strategic and International Studies)**: All analysis, reports, and multimedia content freely accessible [^100^]
- **RAND Corporation**: Core research reports freely available; fee-based services exist for proprietary databases, restricted survey data ($4,000/file), and custom consulting [^428^]
- **Chatham House**: Research publications freely available; membership pays for events, The World Today magazine, International Affairs journal, and eLibrary access -- NOT for basic research [^29^]

**Strategic Implication:** SRC Advisory should follow the "open by default" norm for its core research. Free tier should include substantial research content. Paid differentiation must be built around *access, experience, and exclusivity* -- not basic research availability.

#### 2. Registration Walls: 16x More Effective Than Newsletter Forms

- Registration walls generate **16 times more registrations** than traditional newsletter signup forms, per Salem Reporter's 30-day head-to-head test [^445^]
- **20% of free-registered readers** eventually convert to paid subscriptions [^445^]
- Registered users are **10 times more likely** to become paying customers than anonymous visitors, per Piano research [^445^]
- HBR's model: 2 free articles for anonymous users, registration wall on 3rd article, then 15 articles/month for registered users before hitting paywall [^569^] [^570^]
- The Telegraph's hard registration wall (email required for one premium article/week) helped **triple daily subscriber acquisition** [^543^]

**Strategic Implication:** SRC should implement a registration wall after limited free sampling (e.g., 2-3 articles) rather than relying on passive newsletter signups.

#### 3. Content Types for Free Tier

Based on analysis of think tanks and content platforms, the following content types should be FREE:

| Content Type | Free Tier Access | Rationale |
|-------------|------------------|-----------|
| Short opinion pieces / op-eds | Always free | Awareness-building, SEO, social sharing [^427^] |
| Selected policy briefs (summary) | Free | Demonstrate expertise and value [^473^] |
| Newsletters | Free to all subscribers | Habit formation, engagement driver [^498^] |
| Podcasts | Free (industry standard) | CFR, ECFR, Chatham House all offer free podcasts [^196^] [^10^] |
| Event recordings (selected) | Free for registered users | Conversion trigger for events-based membership |
| Short briefs (<1,000 words) | Free | "Free articles are for reach and advertising" [^427^] |
| Infographics & data visualizations | Free | Shareable, drives brand awareness |

**Always-Premium Content (Gated):**
- Full-length research reports (executive summary free, full report gated)
- Exclusive datasets and proprietary tools (like IISS Military Balance+ at GBP 695/year) [^320^]
- Members-only events and closed-door briefings [^29^]
- Downloadable PDF versions of reports
- Historical archive (deep archive access restricted)
- Ad-free reading experience [^504^]

#### 4. Executive Summary Free / Full Report Paid: Proven Model

- The "Teaser/Full model" (30% of most valuable findings released free, full PDF gated) is widely used [^429^]
- Soft gating (partial access) achieves **34-42% conversion** vs. 15-22% for hard gating [^421^]
- For B2B content, progressive profiling achieves **47-58% conversion** with higher lead quality over time [^421^]
- "3-2-1 method" for progressive profiling: email+name (3 fields), then company+position (2 fields), then budget-timing (1 field) -- achieves **72% higher form completion** [^421^]
- Delayed gating (access for 5-10 minutes or 30-50% of content, then form) increases conversion by **35-45%** compared to hard gates [^421^]

**Strategic Implication:** SRC should offer executive summaries freely, with full PDF reports gated behind registration (free tier) or subscription (premium tier).

#### 5. Metered vs. Freemium for Think Tanks

- **Freemium is the most frequently observed paywall internationally**, with hybrid models on the rise [^201^]
- Pure-metered models fell from 35% to 9% of publishers between 2017-2023 [^201^] [^445^]
- Freemium model gives users control over what they read; metered gives publishers control [^441^]
- For niche/B2B expertise (like security think tanks), **freemium with gated deep dives** is recommended [^440^]
- Freemium allows free users to browse full catalog but only access free-tagged content; metered limits total articles regardless of topic [^440^]

**Strategic Implication:** For SRC, a **hybrid freemium model** is optimal: selected content always free (opinions, briefs, newsletters), premium content (full reports, exclusive analysis) always gated. This gives users clarity on what's free vs. paid while allowing discovery of all topics.

#### 6. Free Tier Access Limits: Recommended Architecture

| Limit Type | Recommendation | Industry Data |
|-----------|----------------|---------------|
| Free articles/month (anonymous) | 2-3 articles | HBR: 2 free before reg wall; Telegraph: 1/week [^569^] [^543^] |
| Articles/month (registered) | 5-10 selected pieces + unlimited opinions/briefs | Registered users get 3-4x more page views [^534^] |
| Archive depth (free) | 6 months rolling | Latest free, deep archive paid is common model [^292^] |
| Newsletter access | Unlimited, free | Key habit-building tool [^498^] |
| Podcast access | Unlimited, free | Industry standard [^196^] |
| Event access | Public events free; closed briefings paid | Chatham House model [^29^] |
| Video clips | Selected clips free; full recordings paid | Teaser model |
| Search/browse | Full catalog visible; premium flagged | Discovery is key for conversion |

#### 7. The "Aha Moment" That Drives Conversion

Conversion triggers that actually work [^161^] [^450^] [^451^]:

- **Activation milestones, not time-based prompts**: Trigger upgrade after users complete meaningful actions (download 3rd report, attend first event, save an article)
- **Value realization moment**: When a user references your research in their own work -- this is the think tank equivalent of SaaS "aha moment"
- **Usage gates**: "You've accessed 90% of your free reports this month" -- loss aversion trigger
- **Exclusive access tease**: Preview of members-only content at key decision moments
- **Social proof at conversion point**: "2,000+ security professionals upgraded this quarter"
- **Trial countdown**: Final 48-72 hours of trial create natural urgency

For think tanks specifically, conversion aha moments likely include:
- Using an SRC insight in a briefing, memo, or policy document
- Attending an SRC event and making a valuable connection
- Discovering unique data or analysis not available elsewhere
- Personalized content recommendation that surfaces a highly relevant report

#### 8. Free User Onboarding Flow: Recommended Welcome Series

Based on best practices from Spotify, HBR, and B2B SaaS [^532^] [^453^]:

| Day | Email Focus | Trigger |
|-----|-------------|---------|
| 0 (immediate) | Welcome + value proposition + "save your first article" | Registration completion |
| 1 | Personalized content recommendations based on signup source/interests | Time-based |
| 3 | Highlight exclusive report (executive summary) + "upgrade for full access" | If user has read 2+ articles |
| 7 | Weekly digest of new research + trending content | Time-based |
| 14 | "What you've accessed this month" + upgrade prompt if engaged | Usage threshold met |
| 21 | Invite to upcoming free event + networking opportunity | Time-based |
| 30 | Monthly summary + "Your peers upgraded for these reasons" + social proof | High-engagement users |

**Key principles:**
- Behavior-based emails get **3-5x higher engagement** than time-based [^451^]
- Don't upsell until user has demonstrated engagement (read 2+ articles) [^532^]
- Spotify model: Don't ask for upgrade until product demonstrates value through personalization [^532^]
- Each email should have one clear call-to-action [^532^]

#### 9. Nurture Strategies for "Registered But Not Paid" Users

- **Segment by engagement**: Not all free users deserve equal attention. High-engagement users (5+ articles/month) get direct sales outreach; low-engagement get automated nurture [^159^]
- **Drip vs. nurture**: Drip campaigns (time-based) for top-of-funnel; nurture campaigns (behavior-based) for middle/bottom funnel [^469^] [^470^]
- **Progressive profiling**: Collect additional data over time -- topic interests, organization type, role -- to personalize conversion offers [^533^]
- **Re-engagement sequences**: Target inactive users (no login in 30 days) with "what you missed" emails [^468^]
- **Community as feeder**: Maintain free community as continuous feeder into paid membership; successful conversions see **10-20% of engaged members upgrade** [^184^]
- **Free workshops that preview premium**: Host free workshops giving sneak peek into premium tier [^184^]

#### 10. Content Freshness Strategy

- **Latest content free, archive paid**: Most common newspaper/archive model; positions fresh analysis as accessible while monetizing historical depth [^292^]
- **Rolling window**: 6-12 months of recent content free; older content requires registration or subscription
- **Exception for evergreen**: Foundational/landmark reports remain free as "audience builders" [^536^]
- **Themed collections**: Curated collections around topics (e.g., "Cybersecurity 2025," "Swiss Security Review") can be gated as premium products [^292^]

**Recommended for SRC:**
- All new publications freely accessible for first 30 days
- Rolling 12-month window for registered free users
- Full archive (5+ years) available to premium subscribers only
- Landmark/flagship reports remain permanently free as audience builders

#### 11. Community Access for Free Users

- **Free commenting access**: Can increase engagement significantly; commenting creates " belonging to a community" which is a registered user benefit [^533^]
- **Network effects**: Products where value compounds with participation pull users toward paid plans organically [^159^]
- **Community as marketing channel**: Free community becomes best sales channel when positioned correctly -- share paid member success stories, premium content previews [^184^]
- **Limitation**: Free community access should exist but premium members get enhanced access (exclusive forums, direct expert access, members-only LinkedIn group like Chatham House) [^29^]
- **15-25% of engaged community members** typically upgrade when conversion is well-positioned [^184^]

#### 12. Search and Discoverability for Free Users

- **Full catalog browsing**: Free users should be able to *see* entire content catalog with clear free/premium labels on each item -- FOMO drives conversion [^236^]
- **Premium content as teaser**: Show first 30-50% of premium article, then gate remainder -- this creates the "value gap" that motivates upgrade [^429^]
- **Discovery features for registered users**: Save for later, personalized recommendations, resume reading -- all increase engagement and create stickiness [^533^] [^570^]
- **HBR model**: Registered users can save articles to "My Library" and get personalized recommendations based on interests -- features that increase engagement before asking for payment [^570^]

### Trends & Signals

1. **Registration walls replacing newsletter forms**: Registration is becoming the standard first step in the conversion funnel, not optional newsletter signup [^445^] [^533^]
2. **Freemium dominates over metering**: Pure metering fell from 35% to 9%; freemium + hybrid models rising [^201^]
3. **First-party data as strategic asset**: With cookie deprecation, registration walls are increasingly about data collection for personalization, not just subscription prep [^533^] [^534^]
4. **Dynamic/hybrid models on the rise**: AI-powered paywalls that adapt to user behavior are the next frontier, but require significant tech investment [^445^]
5. **Events as membership anchor**: For think tanks specifically, events and networking (not content access) are becoming the primary membership value proposition [^29^] [^175^]
6. **Audio content as free gateway**: Podcasts and audio articles are widely free and serve as entry points to paid subscription [^143^]
7. **Personalization as conversion driver**: Publishers that personalize recommendations and user experience see dramatically higher engagement and conversion [^533^] [^570^]

---

### Controversies & Conflicting Claims

1. **Open access vs. monetization tension**: Some argue think tanks should remain fully open to maximize policy impact; others argue sustainability requires monetization [^175^] [^488^]. SRC must balance mission impact with financial sustainability.
2. **Free tier generosity**: Too generous a free tier cannibalizes paid conversion; too stingy kills awareness. The "value dilemma" is real -- offer too much for free and there's little reason to upgrade; offer too little and users bounce [^440^] [^159^]
3. **Registration wall timing**: Early registration walls (before value demonstration) can drive 90%+ traffic drop-offs [^536^]. Late registration walls miss the opportunity to capture engaged users. Optimal timing is critical.
4. **Community access debate**: Free community access builds network effects but may reduce incentive to upgrade. Counter-argument: free community is your best marketing channel, not your business [^184^]
5. **Content freshness vs. archive value**: Some argue the latest content should always be free (drives conversation and relevance); others argue the archive is the real premium asset. Hybrid approaches (rolling window) are emerging as best practice.

#### Appendix A: Recommended SRC Free Tier Architecture

**TIER 0: Anonymous Visitor**
- 2 opinion pieces or briefs per month before registration wall
- Full content catalog browsable (premium items flagged)
- Newsletter signup available (no registration required)

- Full historical archive (5+ years)
- Members-only closed briefings
- Direct expert access (Q&A sessions)
- Ad-free experience
- Priority event registration
- Exclusive members-only newsletter
- Network access (members directory)
- Custom research briefings (top tier)
