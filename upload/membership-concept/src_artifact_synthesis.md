## Part 1: Key Themes Across All Dimensions (10 Themes)

### Theme 1: Open-by-Default Is Non-Negotiable
Every major think tank (Brookings, Carnegie, ECFR, CSIS, RAND, Chatham House) makes core research freely available. Paid tiers differentiate through experience, access, and exclusivity — not basic content gating [^549^] [^554^] [^196^] [^100^] [^428^] [^29^]. SRC must follow this norm or risk irrelevance in the policy discourse.

### Theme 2: The Registration Wall Is the Single Highest-ROI Conversion Lever
Registration walls generate 16x more signups than newsletter forms, and 20% of registered users eventually convert to paid [^445^]. Registered users are 10x more likely to become paying customers than anonymous visitors [^445^]. SRC should invert the traditional funnel: registration wall BEFORE paywall.

### Theme 3: Direct Analyst Access Commands the Highest Willingness-to-Pay
GLG charges $1,500-$2,000/hour for expert consultations [^569^] [^431^]. RANE Enterprise, Oxford Analytica CCM, and Chatham House Major Corporate all use direct analyst access as their top-tier anchor [^59^] [^323^] [^113^]. This is the #1 premium differentiator across all competitors.

### Theme 4: Events (Not Content) Are Becoming the Primary Membership Anchor
Chatham House's Full membership is justified by "up to 100 member events per year" — not by publications [^113^]. CSIS invitation-only groups are entirely about direct interaction [^27^]. SRC should design tiers around EVENT ACCESS frequency, not just content volume.

### Theme 5: Tier Naming Is a Conversion-Critical Decision
"Basic" signals inadequacy and converts 15-30% worse than value-based names (Essential, Core, Professional) [^163^] [^413^]. GCTI's SIGNAL/SENTINEL/SOVEREIGN branding demonstrates creative differentiation works [^208^]. SRC must avoid "Basic" entirely.

### Theme 6: AI Video Briefings Represent a Genuine First-Mover Opportunity
No major think tank has deployed AI avatar (vAvatar) video briefings at scale [^445^] [^533^]. Synthesia supports 175+ languages with 70-99% cost reduction vs. traditional video [^445^]. Switzerland's quadrilingual reality (DE/FR/IT/EN) is a strategic advantage no Anglo think tank can match.

### Theme 7: The Reverse Timewall (Content Cascade) Outperforms Traditional Models
Rather than content going free then archiving, SRC should use a "reverse timewall": Expert gets immediate access → Premium gets +24h → Basic gets +72h → Free gets +2 weeks. BoiseDev's 24h timewall proved this generates revenue without locking the public out [^445^].

### Theme 8: Swiss Neutrality + Privacy Is a Monetizable Differentiator
Switzerland ranks highest GDP/capita globally; consumers normalize elevated prices [^755^]. Strict privacy laws and perceived independence are monetizable differentiators that US/UK think tanks cannot replicate [^964^]. This addresses the #1 critique of Anglo think tanks (perceived bias).

### Theme 9: Annual Billing at 17% Discount Is the Revenue Optimization Sweet Spot
Annual plans retain 92% of customers vs. 68% for monthly — a 24 percentage point difference [^762^]. Annual customers generate 40-71% more lifetime value despite the discount [^760^] [^762^]. Defaulting to annual increases adoption 2-3x [^760^].

### Theme 10: The "Two-Track" Individual + Corporate Architecture Maximizes Revenue
SRC needs parallel but connected tracks: Individual (Free→Essential→Professional→Executive) and Corporate (Team→Department→Enterprise). Corporate members should be NOMINATED individual accounts (Chatham House model) — making the corporate sale about "seats at the table" rather than "content access" [^113^].

## Part 4: Pricing Recommendations with Justification

### 4.1 Recommended Individual Pricing (CHF)

| Tier | Monthly | Annual | Annual Discount | Target Segment |
|------|---------|--------|-----------------|----------------|
| **Free** | CHF 0 | CHF 0 | — | Lead generation, students, browsers |
| **Essential** (formerly Basic) | CHF 29 | CHF 290 | 17% (2 months free) | Young professionals, price-sensitive |
| **Professional** (formerly Premium) | CHF 79 | CHF 790 | 17% (2 months free) | Target tier: serious professionals |
| **Executive** (formerly Expert) | CHF 149 | CHF 1,490 | 17% (2 months free) | Executives, institutional decision-makers |

**Justification:**
- **Essential at CHF 29** positions competitively between GCTI AEGIS Pro ($29/mo) and Geopolitical Desk (~$37/mo), while sitting below NZZ Pro (CHF 45/mo) — appropriate for a lower-tier entry [^208^] [^123^] [^800^]
- **Professional at CHF 79** is 2.7x Essential, exceeding the 1.4x-1.8x benchmark because Essential is deeply limited, not a serious professional tool [^794^]
- **Executive at CHF 149** is 1.9x Professional, making Professional look like the obvious value choice (decoy effect) [^831^] [^834^]
- **Annual discount of 17%** ("2 months free") is the most common and best-converting framing [^759^] [^760^]
- **Swiss VAT of 8.1%** applies to all tiers; prices displayed excluding VAT on page, added at checkout [^777^] [^778^]

### 4.2 Student / Young Professional Discounts

| Tier | Standard | Under 30 | Student |
|------|----------|----------|---------|
| Essential | CHF 29/mo | CHF 24/mo | CHF 19/mo |
| Professional | CHF 79/mo | CHF 49/mo | CHF 39/mo |

**Justification:** Modeled on Chatham House's gold-standard approach: Under 30 at GBP 220 (36% discount) positioned ABOVE Associate at GBP 195 — protecting brand while offering accessibility [^29^].

### 4.3 Corporate Pricing

| Tier | Model | Entry Price |
|------|-------|-------------|
| Essential Team | Per seat (min 3) | CHF 25/seat/month |
| Professional Team | Per seat + volume discounts | CHF 65/seat/month; 10% off at 10+ seats, 20% at 25+ |
| Executive Enterprise | Flat annual license | CHF 5,000-25,000/year |

**Justification:** Per-seat pricing used by ~70% of enterprise SaaS [^761^]. Volume discounts follow standard headcount brackets [^761^]. Executive/Enterprise requires annual invoice (standard for B2B) with QR-bill support [^803^].

### 4.4 Pricing Psychology Tactics

| Tactic | Expected Impact | Source |
|--------|----------------|--------|
| "Most Popular" badge on Professional tier | 50-65% of signups to middle tier | [^817^] |
| Annual as default on pricing page | 2-3x higher annual adoption | [^760^] |

| 10-day free trial (opt-in, no CC) | Better than 30-day (71% better conversion) | [^758^] |

### 5.3 The "Analyst Minutes" Currency Model (Executive Tier)

Expert tier value should be denominated in "analyst minutes" rather than generic "access" [^569^] [^431^]:

| Executive Tier Inclusion | Market Equivalent Value |
|-------------------------|------------------------|
| 60 minutes direct analyst video time annually | GLG: $1,500-$2,500/hr = $1,500-$2,500 value |
| Annual Switzerland summit (2 days) | CFR Annual Dinner equivalent: $5,000+ perceived value |
| Real-time flash alerts (4hr response guarantee) | Oxford Analytica client-only calls: $3,000+/yr value |
| **Total perceived value** | **$5,000-$10,000+** |
| **Executive tier price** | **CHF 1,490 (~$1,790) / year** |
| **Value-to-price ratio** | **3-6x** |

This creates an "irresistible value proposition where the subscription pays for itself in a single call" [^569^].

Every top-tier think tank has an in-person anchor event: CFR Annual Dinner, Atlantic Council Distinguished Leadership Awards, Aspen Ideas Festival, WEF Davos [^432^] [^53^] [^533^]. SRC should create:
